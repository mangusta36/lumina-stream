## Key Findings

1. **Template problem is real:** 5 device guides (smart TV, Android TV box, MAG box, Formuler, Enigma2) are structurally nearly identical. Google sees these as thin content variants.
2. **New content model works:** The 3 rewritten articles + 1 new article score <40% similarity — the question-based structure, data-driven approach, and app-level diagnostic angle are blueprints for fixing remaining issues.
3. **Roku guide is the model:** The Roku article proves a device guide can be unique despite the device — by focusing on the device's limitations rather than repeating generic setup steps.
4. **Niche devices need niche content:** Enigma2, MAG, and Formuler have thin SERPs — articles must go deep, not wide. Template reuse on niche devices is the biggest wasted opportunity.
5. **Hub page overlap is manageable:** The `how-to-setup` hub page overlaps with device guides by design (interlinking). The cannibalization is intentional, not accidental.
6. **Cannibalization is limited:** Only 4 cannibalization pairs exist, down from 8 pre-rewrite. Smart TV + Android TV box is the most critical remaining pair.
7. **EEAT signals are generally absent:** No author bios, no citations from authority domains, no "expert reviewed" markers. This affects trust signals in YMYL-adjacent topics (legality, VPNs).
8. **Internal linking is strong but uneven:** FireStick (21 incoming) and buffering (39 incoming) are link magnets. Niche device guides (Enigma2, Formuler) have 2-3 incoming links each.
